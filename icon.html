62icon

E-commerce Website for artists in Taiwan
which has been developed since 2014

Visit website

Project Brief

62icon is an e-commerce website that empowers independent Taiwanese artists, uploading and selling their original works as 30+ premium consumer goods from Art Prints to Throw Blankets. They create, 62icon produces and fulfills, and every purchase pays an artist.

It builds as a home for artist with all convinient tools. Artists can create their own artspace and sell artworks on consumer goods within few clicks.

Discover & Define

We started with a few meetings and discussions, filled with ideas, high-level requirements and competitive analysis, to get to know their vision for the platform, the associated data and general business goals.

After plenty of discussions and whiteboards full of sketches and notes, once we had synthesized our research it was time to start developing our content strategy. We began by auditing and documenting all of the current site content. This helped to minimize the places where a user could get lost and created a more stream-lined and strategic site architecture.

Design & Development

I started working on wireframes. By keeping the wireframes simple, 62icon team were able to focus on the content and the overall goal of each page without getting bogged down in the details. The wireframes functioned as a sounding board for the project – allowing us to discuss concepts and quickly make revisions when needed.

The 62icon team wanted a design that fun and colorful illustration to convey the message of "a playground for artists". They love the branding direction of using simple and colorful illustions to demonstrate the product.

The wireframes & architecture that we created earlier in the project provided a great foundation for the initial design work. Many iterations was made during design and developing process.

Result

The product is well adopted when it was release in 2015, the business has been preety successful and client are going to lauching a similar website targeting on Chinese market.
